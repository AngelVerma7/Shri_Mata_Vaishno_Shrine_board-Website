Shrine Board

Administration and Management of the Holy Shrine

History of the Shrine

The shrine of Mata Vaishno Devi is considered to be one of the holiest Hindu temples. Legend has it that during the period when the Goddess was staying at Banganga, she performed intense meditation and assumed the form of a rock with three heads (Pindies) representing the three supreme energies.

The cave shrine houses the sacred rock formation representing the three forms of the Mother Goddess - Mahakali, Mahalakshmi, and Mahasaraswati.

Formation of the Shrine Board

The Shri Mata Vaishno Devi Shrine Board was constituted in August 1986 under the Jammu & Kashmir Shri Mata Vaishno Devi Shrine Act, 1988.

Key Objectives:
  • Management and administration of the shrine
  • Development of infrastructure facilities
  • Ensuring smooth pilgrimage experience
  • Maintaining law and order
Board Composition:
  • Governor of J&K (Chairman)
  • Chief Executive Officer
  • Government Representatives
  • Local Representatives
